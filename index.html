01 / Perspective

“I use the language of mechanics to turn complex motion into models we can understand, optimise and control.”

My research connects rigorous mathematical modelling with real engineering systems. The common thread is a geometric and computational approach to motion—whether the system is a dexterous hand, a humanoid, a racing vehicle or a hypoid gearset.

02 / Research directions

Research at the limits
of performance

01

Disturbance-aware trajectory planning

Minimum-time trajectories that propagate uncertainty and enforce probabilistic safety margins on tyre-friction and track-limit constraints.

↘
02

Human-in-the-loop vehicle dynamics

Dynamic driving-simulator experiments with professional drivers to study lap time, steering effort and driving style.

↘
03

Certified nonlinear stability

Lyapunov and Sum-of-Squares methods to estimate safe operating regions for coupled vehicle–driver dynamics.

↘
04

High-fidelity vehicle optimisation

Multibody vehicle models with independent torque, steering, camber and toe control for next-generation race cars.

↘
05

Scalable numerical optimal control

Parallel Schwarz and ADMM decompositions for optimal-control problems with millions of variables.

↘
06

Efficient and quiet transmissions

Multi-objective design of bevel and hypoid gears balancing friction losses, NVH, stresses and manufacturability.

↘

05 / Background

Engineering motion,
across disciplines.

Marco Gabiccini received his Laurea degree with honours and PhD from Università di Pisa in 2000 and 2006. During his doctorate he was a visiting scholar at The Ohio State University GearLab. He joined the Research Center “E. Piaggio” in 2006 and is now a faculty member of the Dipartimento di Ingegneria Civile e Industriale.

His work brings together applied mechanics, robotics and computation, with a long-standing interest in how geometry can clarify—and improve—the behaviour of complex machines.
